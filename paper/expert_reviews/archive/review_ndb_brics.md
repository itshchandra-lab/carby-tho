# NDB/BRICS Institutional Review: The Carbon-Thorium Standard

## Summary Assessment

The paper is analytically sophisticated but has a significant empirical blind spot: the New Development Bank is completely absent. Given that the NDB directly operationalises the transmission mechanisms the paper theorises — Channel 2 (asset recycling) and Channel 3 (credibility) — without requiring capital account opening, this omission substantially weakens the institutional architecture of the India forward prediction.

## Critical Findings

### 1. NDB Completely Absent — Most Important Empirical Omission

**The institution:** The New Development Bank (NDB), established 2014, headquartered in Shanghai.
- Paid-in capital: $10 billion (from 5 founding members)
- Authorised capital: $100 billion (AAA-rated by major agencies)
- Bond issuances in local currencies: INR ($500M, 2021-2024), ZAR, CNY, BRL

**Why it matters for the paper:** The NDB issued INR-denominated bonds in Indian capital markets without requiring capital account liberalisation. This directly addresses the standard objection that India cannot attract reserve demand before opening its capital account: the NDB creates a sovereign-quality rupee asset class through the existing institutional framework.

**Theoretical significance:** Channel 2 (asset recycling) operates through NDB bond holdings. When central banks outside BRICS hold NDB INR bonds, they are accumulating rupee-denominated assets — a form of reserve demand that precedes formal reserve currency status. The paper's claim that "capital account opening must precede reserve demand" is empirically falsified by existing NDB issuance.

**Required addition:** A dedicated subsection (added as §7.6 in this plan) documenting the NDB mechanism. The falsification registry should include: "NDB INR-denominated bond issuance fails to attract non-BRICS sovereign holders by 2028."

---

### 2. India-Russia INR Corridor — Significantly Overstated

**The data:** Of the India-Russia energy trade corridor (~$40-60 billion annual bilateral trade):
- **INR-settled portion**: approximately 5–10% of bilateral trade
- **RUB-settled portion**: approximately 15–20%
- **USD/AED-settled portion**: approximately 70–80% (using UAE dirham as intermediary)
- **Non-convertible holdback**: Russian central bank holds INR in accounts they cannot easily deploy

**Why the gap exists:**
1. Russia has accumulated approximately ₹500 billion in Indian accounts it cannot spend (India's capital account prevents outflows)
2. Russia cannot buy Indian goods at the scale of its energy exports — trade is structurally unbalanced
3. The USD/AED routing emerged because it solves the bilateral imbalance problem

**What the corridor proves:** Trade route exists (Channel 1 activated in bilateral context). What it does not prove: monetary leverage translation through Channels 2 and 3.

**Required qualification:** The paper must add: "approximately 5–10% of INR-RUB corridor is settled in INR; the majority routes through USD or AED intermediaries. The corridor demonstrates route creation; monetary power translation has not followed."

---

### 3. Contingent Reserve Arrangement (CRA) — Not Modelled

**The institution:** BRICS CRA, $100 billion liquidity facility, operational since 2015.
- 30-day liquidity support for member states facing short-term BoP stress
- Linked to IMF programme (70% of allocation requires IMF programme)
- Functions as a speed parameter for Channel 3 activation

**Why it matters:** A central bank considering holding INR reserves faces a credibility hurdle: "Will India maintain monetary stability under BoP stress?" The CRA provides an institutional backstop that reduces this credibility risk premium. It does not provide reserve status itself, but it lowers the threshold for Channel 3 to activate.

**Required addition:** Mention CRA in the NDB section as a speed parameter for Channel 3. No separate falsification condition needed — the CRA's relevance is conditional on the NDB institutional path.

---

### 4. BRICS Pay / mBridge — Emerging Infrastructure

**Additional context:** BRICS Pay (multilateral settlement) and the BIS mBridge project (multi-CBDC platform) are in development as of 2025. If operational, these would directly reduce the dollar dependency that currently routes even BRICS bilateral trade through dollar intermediaries.

**Note for paper:** These are too nascent for quantitative claims but worth acknowledging in a footnote as institutional developments that could alter the timeline of Channel 1 activation if they achieve operational scale.

## Assessment of India Forward Prediction With NDB Incorporated

| Without NDB | With NDB |

| Channel 2 requires full KAOPEN change | Channel 2 already partially operational via NDB INR bonds |
| Prediction dependent entirely on FEMA amendment | Prediction has an institutional bypass pathway |

The India prediction becomes considerably more robust when the NDB is incorporated as an institutional bypass for Channel 2. The capital account constraint remains binding for full Channel 1 activation, but the NDB demonstrates that reserve demand can build before that constraint is removed.

## Overall Assessment

The NDB omission is the paper's most significant institutional gap. The correction requires adding approximately three paragraphs (§7.6 as proposed) and two falsification registry rows. The India-Russia corridor correction requires one paragraph qualification. Both are straightforward to implement and strengthen rather than weaken the paper's theoretical coherence.
