## I. GENUINE STRENGTHS (Panel consensus)

Every reviewer independently acknowledged these contributions:

1. **Three-register architecture** — genuinely novel epistemological design
2. **Japan Fukushima natural experiment** — cleanest identification in the present register (though see critiques)
3. **Falsification registry (§8.3)** — praised by all nine reviewers as a methodological virtue rare in geoeconomics
4. **Relegating pooled OLS to appendix** — appropriate methodological humility given the USA-driven result
5. **GS > 1 distinction** — original, operationalisable, recoverable by independent researchers
6. **Katzenstein filter as theoretical discriminator** — productive direction, though under-specified (see below)

## II. CROSS-CUTTING CRITIQUES (raised by 4+ reviewers independently)

### CRITIQUE 1 — The Paper Is a Single-Country Study Wearing a General Theory's Clothes
*Raised by: USA, India, EU, Japan, UK*

The jackknife result (removing USA reverses coefficient from +84 to −3) is honestly disclosed but not fully confronted. The paper oscillates between claiming generality and disclaiming it. β=84.0 from a reserve currency hegemon is applied to India — a challenger with a closed capital account (KAOPEN = −1.254), 1.6% FX turnover, and a nuclear programme 14 years behind schedule — without defending the coefficient transfer.

> **Required:** Choose one register: own the single-country origin and position comparative cases as consistency checks, not confirmations.

---

### CRITIQUE 2 — Present Register Causal Identification Fails the US Case
*Raised by: USA, EU, Japan*

USD reserve share stabilised at 58.5–59% from 2021. Simultaneously: Fed rate hike cycle (near-zero → 5.25–5.50%), post-COVID flight-to-safety, Russia/Ukraine energy spike increasing dollar-invoiced commodity values, REER appreciation mechanically compressing non-dollar shares. The paper controls for none of these. Without a mechanism trace or placebo test, β=84.0 is a correlation, not a causal estimate.

> **Required:** Either mechanism trace (petrodollar recycling flows specifically increased in 2021–2024) or explicit acknowledgement that the US case is "consistent with" rather than "confirmed by" the energy mechanism.

---

### CRITIQUE 3 — Katzenstein Filter Is Asserted, Not Argued (Four Countries, No Mechanism)
*Raised by: Canada, Australia, Japan, UK*

The filter is applied to four countries with a single assertion: "security integration → energy leverage channelled through dollar system." No institutional pathway is specified for any of the four. Japan's subordination has a documented mechanism (Yoshida Doctrine, MOF anti-reserve policy). Canada, Australia, and UK have no equivalent documented bargain. Five Eyes is an intelligence-sharing arrangement; it does not determine exchange rate regimes.

> **Required:** For each filtered country, specify the institutional pathway. For Canada: CUSMA proportionality clause. For Japan: MOF anti-reserve policy. For Australia: why AUKUS prevents invoicing norm changes. For UK: City of London-based reasoning that doesn't rely on intelligence sharing.

---

### CRITIQUE 4 — WGI Is the Wrong Institutional Quality Proxy
*Raised by: China, Russia, EU*

WGI measures liberal-democratic governance. Kurtz & Schrank (2007, *World Politics*) showed it correlates with GDP per capita and embeds Western institutional biases. For the MPI's purpose — can this state convert energy sovereignty into monetary credibility? — the relevant dimensions are contract enforcement in energy transactions, financial regulatory capacity, central bank credibility, and bilateral treaty enforcement. Russia's Rosatom has an unblemished construction completion record but WGI = 0.40. China has the world's most advanced cross-border payment infrastructure but WGI = 0.56.

> **Required:** Sensitivity analysis using ICRG financial risk ratings or equivalent monetary-credibility-specific proxies.

---

### CRITIQUE 5 — The AUKUS Technical Error
*Raised by: Australia, Canada*

The paper predicts a "step-change at approximately 2035 via AUKUS." AUKUS submarines are nuclear-propelled (HEU from US/UK for naval propulsion), not nuclear-powered for electricity grids. They use zero thorium. The HEU supply arrangement was specifically designed to prevent Australia from developing a sovereign fuel cycle — this was an IAEA negotiation centrepiece. AUKUS makes Australia more, not less, dependent on the US nuclear supply chain.

> **Required:** Correct the technical description. Either remove the 2035 claim or construct an explicit argument for how AUKUS *indirectly* creates conditions for civil nuclear capacity.

---

### CRITIQUE 6 — GS = 2.79 Is Methodologically Fragile
*Raised by: EU (most detailed), Canada, USA, UK*

- CV computed from N=3 annual observations (Phase I: 2005–2007). With 2 degrees of freedom, confidence intervals are enormous — the precision of "2.79" is an artefact.
- RGGI comparison (2009–2012) occurs during GFC demand suppression; RGGI also had a built-in price floor from inception.
- Oil benchmark CV (0.292) stated as "2005–2024" but used to evaluate a 2005–2007 period.
- Post-2021 EUA surge to €90 ignored entirely.

> **Required:** Recompute GS using monthly EUA data (N≈32). Report bootstrap confidence intervals. Specify oil benchmark as contemporaneous with ETS period. Run Bai-Perron multiple structural break test alongside ZA.

---

### CRITIQUE 7 — China Receives 4 Sentences for the Most Important Contemporary Case
*Raised by: China, Russia, India, Canada*

China is the world's largest energy importer, the most aggressive de-dollarisation actor, the operator of TMSR-LF1 (more advanced toward grid connection than India's PFBR), and the primary contemporary challenge to dollar hegemony. TMPI = 4.2 vs India = 15.8 is almost entirely a thorium reserve ranking (2.1% vs 16.1%). China has 57 operating reactors + 22 under construction vs India's 6,780 MW installed. There is no China falsification condition in §8.3.

> **Required:** A dedicated China section (minimum 2,000 words); trajectory-weighted TMPI comparison with India; a China falsification condition; engagement with CIPS/e-CNY as Channel 1 bypass of capital account openness.

## III. COUNTRY-SPECIFIC CRITICAL FINDINGS (unique per reviewer)

### INDIA
- **Capital account liberalisation is politically constrained by a structural coalition** (export interests + state banks + agriculture + RBI bureaucratic interest), not just RBI conservatism. Nuclear credibility gains don't automatically overcome this coalition.
- **INR FX share was flat 2019–2022 despite $1tn GDP growth.** The paper predicts acceleration where the baseline shows stagnation.
- **20% nuclear by 2035 is aspirational** — NPCIL projects 22 GW by 2031 under the most optimistic scenario; the model requires 65–80 GW.
- **Trapped ruble Vostro accounts** from Russian oil purchases are a sanctions anomaly, not a step toward INR internationalisation.
- **Missing:** Gopinath (2015) on dominant currency paradigm stickiness; DAE/BARC Stage 3 timeline documents.

### CHINA
- **TMSR-LF1 is more advanced toward grid connection than PFBR.** The TMPI = 4.2 vs 15.8 ranking is dominated by the reserve share variable (2.1% vs 16.1%), not operational nuclear capability.
- **China is activating Channel 1 without capital account opening** through CIPS + e-CNY + INE yuan crude futures — directly testing and partially refuting the paper's Channel 1 formulation.
- **BRI is simultaneously Strange's four structures** (knowledge, production, finance, security) — the most deliberate attempt to shift all four since the US did so after 1944.
- **Missing:** Prasad (2017) *Gaining Currency*; Liao and McDowell (2015) on PBOC swap lines; Subacchi (2017) *People's Money*.

### JAPAN
- **Three simultaneous confounds** undermine the "cleanest identification" claim: CA surplus collapse (3.7%→0.5% GDP 2010–2013), BOJ QQE deliberately engineering yen depreciation from 2013, and MOF's explicit anti-reserve currency policy since the 1970s.
- **MOF is a direct rival theory** — Grimes (2003) and Lipscy (2003) document that Japan's monetary authorities worked for decades to *prevent* the reserve currency status the paper treats as unexplained.
- **Samuels *3.11*** — the definitive political analysis of Fukushima's policy consequences — is absent from a paper making Fukushima its primary natural experiment.
- **Japan GX plan (¥150tn) is invisible to TMPI** — hybrid energy sovereignty through SMRs, hydrogen, ammonia co-firing is unscored.

### AUSTRALIA
- **AUKUS technical error** — HEU propulsion ≠ civil nuclear energy; AUKUS prevents sovereign fuel cycle development by design.
- **Fossil fuel NEP cliff unmodelled** — IEA projects coal demand peak ~2023, LNG demand peak ~2030. Australian NEP deteriorates sharply in the 2030s. The model should predict AUD reserve *decline* in the 2030–2040 window. The paper doesn't make this prediction.
- **Strongly positive present-era NEP** should predict AUD reserve share *growth* under the paper's own mechanism, but the paper predicts stagnation. These can't both be right without explanation.

### CANADA
- **CANDU is the world's most thorium-compatible commercial reactor technology** — India's Stage 1 PHWR is a direct CANDU derivative. The TMPI treats nuclear capacity as quantity, not quality of thorium compatibility, systematically underestimating Canada's forward position.
- **CUSMA proportionality clause (Chapter 32, Annex 32-D)** — Canada's legal obligation to maintain energy exports to the US is the precise mechanism constraining energy sovereignty → monetary independence. It is never mentioned.
- **Helleiner is completely absent** — Canada was the first country to float its exchange rate in the postwar era (1950–1962), predating general floating by over a decade. This history directly contradicts the "passively subordinated to US" framing.
- **No Canada falsification condition in §8.3** despite an explicit named negative prediction.

### USA
- **Petrodollar system is unwinding during the paper's evidence window** — Saudi yuan oil transactions, OPEC+ fractures, frozen Russian reserves ($300bn). β=84.0 may be a declining structural parameter, not a stable one.
- **US nuclear fleet is aging and declining** (8.4%→7.6% nuclear share, fleet average age 41 years). The scenario analysis should include a US nuclear decline scenario where India closes the TMPI gap substantially.
- **Braun on Fed swap lines** — Fed swap line allocation is politically constructed (allies get access, others don't) and is the dominant current mechanism of dollar hegemony extension. GS > 1 in the paper's own terms, applied to the dollar system itself. Absent from the framework.

### EU
- **EUR reserve share fell 5 percentage points (24%→19%)** but applying β=84.0 to eurozone NEP decline of -0.005 predicts only **-0.42 percentage points**. The energy mechanism explains ~8% of observed EUR decline. The debt crisis explains the rest. This decomposition must be done.
- **CBAM** (fully operational 2026) is external governance projection power — a condition of market access for €2.5tn in annual imports — that the framework cannot model.
- **NGEU (€750bn)** created the first genuine pan-European safe asset, partially resolving the structural constraint on EUR reserve accumulation independently of energy.

### UK
- **Sterling's founding narrative is multi-causal**: WWII debt (£3.5bn blocked balances), Marshall Plan conditions (1947 convertibility crisis), Suez 1956 (US security coercion), 1967 devaluation (military overstretch). Energy invoicing was one contributing factor, not the structural driver.
- **The City of London is untheorised**: 38% of global FX daily turnover. If Switzerland (< 1% of global FX) is an institutional substitution case, the City of London requires at minimum a paragraph's worth of engagement. GBP at 4.5% reserves with TMPI = 0 is the framework's most glaring empirical anomaly.
- **The quantitative test covers the wrong period**: COFER begins 1995; sterling was already near its floor. The narrative claims the 1950–1980 mechanism; the regression tests 1995–2024. These must be distinguished.
- **Brexit = natural experiment ignored**: UK ETS vs EU ETS comparison is precisely what the backward register methodology calls for.

### RUSSIA
- **Rosatom has the world's dominant nuclear knowledge structure** — 70% global uranium enrichment capacity, 40% fuel fabrication, 30+ construction contracts. BREST-OD-300 is the world's most advanced closed fuel cycle programme. TMPI = 9.3 is a severe underestimate of Russia's nuclear knowledge structure position.
- **Ruble-rupee corridor has largely stalled** (India shifted back to dollar pricing through UAE intermediaries by 2023–2024). This is a partial falsification of Mechanism A's sufficiency conditions.
- **Military spending is crowding out Channel 2** — Russia is spending 6–7% GDP on military (30% of federal budget by 2024); NWF assets drawn down for deficit finance, not monetary infrastructure investment.
- **BRICS Kazan 2024 settlement unit proposal** directly operationalises the paper's energy-to-monetary-power conversion hypothesis and is entirely absent from the analysis.

## V. EDITORIAL VERDICT

**This paper has a real, important, publishable theoretical contribution.** The MPI framework is original. The GS measure is operationalisable. The India prediction is the kind of scholarly bet geoeconomics needs. The falsification registry is exemplary.

**In its current form, it would be rejected at *International Organization*, *International Security*, *Review of International Political Economy*, or *China Quarterly*** — not because the thesis is wrong, but because:

1. The founding historical narrative (sterling) is deployed selectively and incompletely
2. The most important contemporary case (China) has 4 sentences
3. The paper's best quantitative evidence (the US case) fails basic confound discipline
4. A technical error on AUKUS will be caught by any reviewer familiar with the programme
5. The coefficient β=84.0 is applied across fundamentally different institutional contexts without defence

**The good news:** Every single one of these is fixable. None requires abandoning the core thesis. The paper needs to be more honest about what it is — a bold theoretical framework with strong evidence in two cases (US present-era, Japan natural experiment) and a specific forward bet (India) — rather than claiming generality it cannot yet sustain. Fix the China section, fix the AUKUS error, recompute GS on monthly data, and this paper has a legitimate path to a top-5 IPE journal.
