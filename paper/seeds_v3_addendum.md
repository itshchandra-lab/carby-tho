# Seeds v3 Addendum: Canada, Australia, Brazil Addition

## Why These Three Matter

The standard de-dollarization conversation features only China, yuan, euro, 
maybe rupee. Adding Australia, Canada, and Brazil to the TMPI cross-section 
produces the most provocative empirical claim in the paper.

## What This Proves

The TMPI ranking is not just a list. It's a diagnostic tool that shows 
WHY some countries with large reserves won't convert them into monetary 
leverage, and which specific constraint is binding.

- Australia: reserves ✓, capacity ✗, institutions ✓ → AUKUS changes this
- Brazil: reserves ✓, capacity ✗, institutions ✗ → double constraint
- Canada: reserves ~, capacity ✓, institutions ✓ → already a reserve currency (AUD/CAD)

## The Genuinely Provocative Claim

AUD and CAD are ALREADY nontraditional reserve currencies:
- AUD: 6.4% of global FX turnover (BIS 2022) — 7th most traded currency
- CAD: 6.2% of global FX turnover (BIS 2022) — 8th most traded currency
- Both confirmed as reserve currencies by Arslanalp et al. (2022)

The claim: if thorium becomes the dominant energy input of the 2040s, 
Australia and Canada are better positioned than China or the US to anchor 
a new energy-monetary arrangement — not because they're powerful, but 
because their energy geography aligns with the next era.

This is falsifiable. Check it in 2035.

## The Geopolitical Alignment Tension (Paper 3)

Both AUS and CAN are US-aligned (Five Eyes). Their thorium leverage doesn't 
translate into monetary independence from the dollar system the way India's 
might. TMPI predicts potential. Whether potential gets activated depends on 
geopolitical alignment.

India will activate. Australia probably won't. Brazil can't (capital controls).
Canada might — quietly, through resource trade denominated in CAD.

That divergence between TMPI potential and actual activation is Paper 3.
